How does different apps sell our data?

Shilpa K. S.
We all know that data collection/tracking is happening regardless of the software whether it’s Android/iOS. Many Applications access our personal information, this can be helpful but it's definitely an invasion of our privacy. Some of the questions that may come to our mind are
Who is buying and selling your data?

Banks, Insurance Companies, Businesses that have products to sell are increasingly interested in our personal data. Data marketing firms deliver so-called data brokers to collect all kinds of data. They analyze and package the data and sell it to businesses and advertisers. We can consider an Example like a company that has 1000,000 monthly active users and the third party pays Rs.10 per user. If the selling company gives data of 1000 users per month, the company can earn up to Rs.10, 000 per month. It's very interesting that users have to accept location tracking, without that it should not be possible for companies to track you. The problem is that most applications inform you that you are using location data but no app says they are sharing it with third parties.

What happens to your data once sold?

Once they buy our data, data brokers create profiles through so-called Data Mining. Most data management organizations combine 2 or more techniques to reach a result as accurately as possible. Some of the techniques are Association This is one of the most widely used data mining techniques. It’s also known as the Relation Technique because it focuses on the relationship between two different data sets. Clustered In these techniques, different data information is grouped together in a single cluster. If data brokers use clustering on our data they get a unique user profile that is used to push targeted products and services

Are there any privacy-friendly Applications out there?

If you don't want your data to be collected something you can do Use VPN(Virtual Private Network) Don't forget to clear your cache and cookies regularly There are some privacy-friendly Apps that do not collect your data. Here are two of them you may feel handy for your daily life. WIRE which was launched in 2014.Since its launch WIRE has proven itself as a safe alternative to establishing messaging service. This App is available for both smartphones and computers. DuckDuckGo is available as an App and as a search engine which means we can use it with other browsers like Google Chrome, Firefox, Safari etc. This app doesn't use cookies to track you on the web. In fact, it does not collect any personal details at all. It automatically hides your IP address from the site you visit.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 c. unsalted butter - room temperature
  • 1 c. granulated sugar
  • 3 egg whites
  • 2 tsp. vanilla extract
  • 1 3/4 c. cake flour
  • 2/3 c. sliced freeze dried strawberries - pureed into powder

    		•  Instructions

    1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Line a standard size cupcake pan with liners, set aside.
    2. In the bowl of a stand mixer (or a large mixing bowl with a hand mixer), beat the butter and sugar until light and fluffy, about 2-3 minutes. Add the egg whites one at a time, mixing after each addition. Add the vanilla extract, mix to combine.
    3. In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the cake flour, powdered strawberries, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. With mixing speed on low, gradually alternate adding the dry ingredients along with the sour cream and milk.
    4. After all of the ingredients have been added, beat on medium for 15 seconds. Gently fold in diced strawberries. Using a large cookie scoop (#20 or 3.5 tablespoons), fill each of the liners with the batter.
    5. Place in the oven and bake for 18-22 minutes or until the centers are set. You can use a toothpick or cake tester to ensure that they are done. Remove from the oven and allow to cool in the pan for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. Meanwhile, to make the frosting, in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the whisk attachment (or a large mixing bowl using a hand mixer and the whisk attachment), beat the butter until smooth and creamy, about 1 minute.
